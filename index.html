Some common questions
were often asked

What breeds are good with children?

Golden Retriever: Similar to Labs, Golden Retrievers are known for their gentle and tolerant nature. They are highly intelligent and easily trainable, making them great companions for children.

How often should I bathe my dog?

Activity level: Dogs that spend a lot of time outdoors or engage in activities that make them dirty, such as swimming or rolling in the mud, may need more frequent bathing. In such cases, bathing every 2-4 weeks may be necessary.

How often should I take my dog to the veterinarian?

Puppies: When you first bring a new puppy home, they require a series of vaccinations to protect against common diseases. Typically, puppies receive a series of vaccinations every 3 to 4 weeks until they are around 16 weeks old. During this time, you should schedule regular visits with your veterinarian to ensure your puppy is growing properly and to address any health concerns.

What are the signs of a healthy dog?

Hydration: A healthy dog will be well-hydrated. The gums should be moist and pink, and if you press on the gums, the color should return quickly. Dehydration can be indicated by dry or pale gums.

How can I socialize my dog with other dogs and people?

Start with basic obedience training: Teach your dog basic commands like sit, stay, and come. This will establish a foundation for their training and make it easier to control their behavior during socialization.

How long can a dog be left alone?

Puppies: Puppies require more attention and care than adult dogs. They have limited bladder control and may need to relieve themselves frequently. A general rule of thumb is that a puppy can hold its bladder for about one hour per month of age. For example, a 3-month-old puppy should not be left alone for more than three hours at a time.
